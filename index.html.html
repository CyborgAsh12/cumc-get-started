🟢 Getting Started as a Beginner

1 First Steps - Your First Climbing Session

Start with the Monday Bouldering Social:

  • Mondays 7-9pm at Kelsey Kerridge bouldering wall
  • Followed by a trip to the pub to meet other members
  • No experience needed - everyone starts here
  • £0 if you're a member, £6 if not (one free try-out session available)

What to expect: Friendly, supportive atmosphere. Experienced climbers will show you the basics. No pressure, just learning.

2 Essential Gear to Start

Good news: You don't need much to get started. CUMC has rental gear available!

What CUMC Provides (Free for Members)

  • ✓ Harness (rock climbing)
  • ✓ Rope & carabiners
  • ✓ Climbing guidebooks
  • ✓ Crash pads (bouldering)
  • View all available gear →

What You Should Buy

  • ✓ Climbing shoes (£70-150)
  • ✓ Chalk and chalk bag (£10-20)
  • ✓ Brush for holds (£5)
  • ✓ Eventually: your own harness (£30-80)
  • Gear advice from CUMC →

3 Become a Member

Cost: £30 per academic year (or £25 if you join in Easter term)

What membership includes:

  • Free access to all club equipment (ropes, harnesses, crash pads, guidebooks)
  • Reduced rates on club meets (usually £6 indoor, £15 outdoor)
  • BMC (British Mountaineering Council) insurance included
  • Access to exclusive social events and outdoor trips
  • Club stash (hoodies, t-shirts, etc.)

How to join: Create an account on the CUMC website and sign up in the Membership section →

4 Your First Club Meet

Non-members can attend one meet for free to try it out!

Check out our regular meets:

  • Freshers' Meets (October): Perfect for new climbers. Bouldering, sports climbing, and social events
  • Indoor Meets: Climb at various UK climbing gyms throughout term
  • Outdoor Meets: Weekend trips to climbing areas (Stanage Edge, Peak District, etc.)

View upcoming meets and sign up →

5 What Happens Next

Month 1: Focus on technique. Come to socials, learn proper footwork and body positioning. Don't worry about difficulty.

Month 2-3: Increase frequency to 3-4 sessions/week. Join an indoor meet. Start climbing harder routes. Build your foundation.

Month 4+: Consider your next steps. Want to try outdoor climbing? Interested in trad or sport climbing? This is when you specialise.

Remember: Everyone starts as a beginner. Be patient with yourself. Progress comes naturally with consistent, smart training.

🏔️ Join CUMC Meets - Prerequisites & What You Need

Different meets have different requirements. Here's what you need for each:

🏢 Indoor Gym Meets

What they are: Climbing at indoor gyms (Kelsey Kerridge, Rainbow Rocket, etc.)

✓ Prerequisites:

  • • At least 2-3 climbing sessions experience
  • • Comfortable with basic footwork and grip
  • • Know how to warm up safely
  • • For top-rope: Must know how to belay
  • • For bouldering: No belay needed, just courage!

📚 Learn to Belay:

  • • Top Rope Belay Setup →
  • • The Climbing Doctor (Safety) →
  • • Ask experienced climbers at Monday socials

💰 Cost:

£6 per person if non-member, free if member

🏔️ Outdoor Climbing Meets

What they are: Weekend trips to UK crags (Stanage, Peak District, etc.)

✓ Prerequisites:

  • • At least 3+ months climbing experience
  • • Strong belay skills (mandatory)
  • • Comfortable on outdoor rock
  • • Suitable for: Bouldering, Sport, or Trad
  • • Your own shoes recommended
  • • Bring climbing guidebook or download beta

🧗 Learn Sport or Trad:

  • • Lead Climbing Techniques →
  • • Intro to Trad Climbing →
  • • Get mentored by experienced members

💰 Cost:

£15 per person typically (covers transport, gear, etc.)

🧗 Bouldering-Specific Meets

What they are: Dedicated bouldering sessions at crags or gyms

✓ Prerequisites:

  • • Beginner friendly! Just 1-2 months OK
  • • No belay skills needed
  • • You spot for others (learn how)
  • • Bring crash pad for outdoor bouldering
  • • Wear appropriate clothing (no loose items)
  • • Good footwear recommended

📚 Learn Spotting:

  • • How to Spot Climbers →
  • • Ask at Monday socials (super friendly!)
  • • Practice with experienced climbers first

💰 Cost:

£6 indoor, £15 outdoor typically

🪂 Rope Climbing (Sport & Trad)

Key difference: Sport = pre-placed bolts, Trad = you place gear

Sport Climbing Prerequisites:

  • • 3+ months experience minimum
  • • Strong top-rope belaying skills
  • • Know how to clip carabiners
  • • Comfortable leading indoors first
  • • Learn to fall safely (practice with partner)

Trad Climbing Prerequisites:

  • • 12+ months climbing experience
  • • Expert sport climbing skills first
  • • Formal trad course required (£100-200)
  • • Learn gear placement & anchor building
  • • Mentorship from experienced member

🏥 Injury Prevention Essentials

  • ✓ Warm-up: 10-15 mins before every session
  • ✓ Perfect form first: Before increasing difficulty
  • ✓ Progressive loading: +10% per week max
  • ✓ Listen to pain: Sharp pain = stop
  • ✓ Rest days: 2-3 complete rest days/week
